“And now...a story about a very strange lion—in fact, the strangest lion I have ever met.” So begins Shel Silverstein’s very first children’s book, Lion Who Shot Back, in 1963.

A picture from Silverstein's book: Lion Who Shot Back


Shel Silverstein was an American writer, poet, cartoonist, songwroter, and playwright. Silverstein was drafted into the United States Army where he served in Japan and Korea. His first works were published in newspapers and magazines. He became the leading cartoonist in Playboy magazine.




Silverstein wrote songs that were hits for other artists. He composed origional music for several films, playing a variety of instraments. He worked on a comedy play: Look, Charlie: A Short History of the Pratfall that later lead him to write more than 100 one-act plays.

He has writen many books over the years. The book A Light in the Attic was dedicated to his daughter that died at age 11 from a cerebral aneyrsm. Silverstein died of a heart attack in 1999 at the age of 68. Sadly, two decades after his death, he became one of the hundreds of artists who's music was destroyed in the 2008 Universal Studios fire.
